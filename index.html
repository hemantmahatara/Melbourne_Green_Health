The Story

Scroll through key findings from the 2024 Royal Botanic Gardens Victoria assessment of every garden bed in Melbourne.

01

The Full Picture

4,345 shrub beds were assessed across the municipality. Nearly 36.5% are in poor condition or total failure — requiring urgent renewal investment.

02

Where Renewal Is Urgent

Highlighted in red: beds in poor condition or failure. Royal Park, Kensington and West Melbourne have high concentrations of degraded beds.

03

Exotic Dominance

35% of beds are dominated by exotic species — not native to Australia. Exotic beds actually have the best average condition ratings, raising questions about maintenance vs. ecological value.

04

Indigenous Beds Under Pressure

Beds planted with indigenous species (plants native to greater Melbourne) show the worst condition outcomes — 50% rated poor or failure. These ecologically valuable beds need urgent support.

05

Most Common Plant

Lomandra longifolia (Mat-rush) appears in 17.3% of all beds — the most planted species in Melbourne's public gardens. Indigenous grass-like perennials dominate the top 10.

06

Stormwater Gardens

143 bioretention beds across the city filter stormwater runoff. These specialised plantings form part of Melbourne's water-sensitive urban design strategy.

07

A Call to Action

The data reveals a city at a crossroads: invest now in renewal, or face accelerating degradation of Melbourne's green infrastructure. Explore the map to find beds in your neighbourhood.
